Why Philippine SMEs Need a Dedicated Ecommerce Platform

The Philippine ecommerce market grew 28% year-on-year through 2025, with SMEs now accounting for over 40% of online retail sales. However, many Filipino sellers still rely on Facebook Shops or Lazada commission structures that limit profitability and brand control.

A dedicated ecommerce platform gives you:

  • Full brand ownership — your own domain, design control, customer data
  • Lower take rates — avoid 5-15% marketplace commissions on every sale
  • Direct customer relationships — email marketing, repeat purchases, loyalty programs
  • International expansion — accept payments from buyers across ASEAN and beyond
  • Integration with logistics partners — automated fulfillment with LBC, J&T, and 2GO

This guide compares the five best platforms for Philippine SMEs, evaluated on pricing in PHP, payment gateway support, shipping integrations, and ease of use.

1. Shopify: The Best All-in-One Solution for Philippine SMEs

Shopify is the global market leader, and its Philippine offering is specifically tailored for local sellers. The Basic plan starts at ₱599/month (USD $11 equivalent, so PHP pricing reflects fair localization).

Why Shopify Wins in the Philippines

  • GCash and PayMaya integration — the two dominant local payment methods for Filipinos
  • Peso pricing — transparent PHP billing with no surprise FX conversions
  • Drag-and-drop themes — no coding required; 100+ mobile-optimized templates
  • Built-in shipping integrations — partnerships with LBC, J&T, Lalamove, and 2GO
  • Multi-channel selling — sell on Facebook, TikTok Shop, and Instagram alongside your Shopify store
  • Inventory management — real-time stock sync across channels; 99.99% uptime
  • Professional apps ecosystem — thousands of free and paid extensions for SMS notifications, loyalty programs, and email marketing

✓ Pros

  • Fastest checkout (avg. 2-second page load)
  • Industry-leading 24/7 support in English
  • 1-click software updates; no maintenance
  • Trusted by 5M+ sellers globally
  • Unlimited product listings
  • Free SSL certificate & PCI compliance

✗ Cons

  • Transaction fees (2.9% + ₱15) if using Shopify Payments
  • Premium themes can cost ₱200-800
  • App ecosystem adds costs (₱50-2,000/month)
  • Not ideal for marketplace-only sellers (Lazada, Shopee)

Pricing Breakdown for Philippine SMEs

  • Basic Plan (₱599/month) — 2 staff, up to 10 products per vendor, basic reports
  • Standard Plan (₱1,999/month) — 5 staff, advanced reports, gift cards, abandoned cart recovery
  • Premium Plan (₱4,999/month) — unlimited staff, advanced fraud detection, priority support

Payment processing costs: GCash and PayMaya charge 2.5-3% per transaction. Shopify Payments adds 2.9% + ₱15 per online sale, but removes the need for a separate merchant account.

2. Shoplazza: The Affordable Alternative

Shoplazza is a rising platform backed by ByteDance (TikTok parent company) with aggressive pricing in Southeast Asia. Its ₱299/month Starter plan appeals to new sellers testing the waters.

Strengths: fast setup (30 minutes), TikTok Shop native integration, excellent mobile UX, and local payment support. Weaknesses: smaller app ecosystem than Shopify, less mature inventory management, and limited email marketing tools built-in.

Best for: Sellers with 50-500 SKUs who prioritize TikTok Shop integration and lower upfront costs. Not recommended if you plan to scale beyond ₱100K/month in sales.

3. BigCommerce: Enterprise-Grade for Large Catalogs

BigCommerce's Plus plan (₱1,199/month) is built for SMEs with 1,000+ products or B2B operations (wholesale + retail). It offers white-label options and advanced APIs for custom integrations.

When to choose BigCommerce: You have a large team, complex fulfillment workflows, or need to sell to both retail and wholesale customers simultaneously. Setup time: 1-2 weeks with mandatory onboarding.
