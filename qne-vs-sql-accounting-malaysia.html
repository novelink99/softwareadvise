QNE vs SQL Accounting Malaysia: Which Fits Your SME?

Updated 2026-04-30 · 7 min read · Malaysia

Verdict

SQL

SQL Accounting edges ahead for most Malaysian SMEs due to stronger GST compliance, superior local bank feed integration, and better mobile support. QNE remains competitive for very small operations prioritizing cost, but SQL's feature depth and local accounting standards alignment make it the stronger choice for growth-focused businesses.

Why Compare These Two?

Malaysia's small and medium enterprise sector is booming, with over 900,000 registered SMEs contributing 37% of GDP. Yet many still rely on spreadsheets for accounting. QNE and SQL Accounting are two of the most accessible cloud-based solutions marketed to Malaysian businesses, each claiming to simplify bookkeeping, invoicing, and tax compliance.

But which one actually delivers? This comparison cuts through marketing claims to help you decide based on your business needs, budget, and compliance requirements under Malaysian tax law, including Goods and Services Tax (GST) principles and Companies Commission of Malaysia (SSM) filing obligations.

QNE: What You're Getting

QNE is a lightweight, affordable accounting software positioned for micro-businesses and sole proprietors in Malaysia. It emphasizes simplicity and low cost, making it attractive to startups with minimal accounting needs.

Key Features

  • Basic invoicing: Create, send, and track invoices with local invoice numbering
  • Expense tracking: Simple receipt upload and categorization
  • Profit & loss reports: Month-end P&L generation aligned to Malaysian financial year (Jan–Dec)
  • Tax calculation: Partial GST calculation support, though requires manual cross-check
  • Mobile app: Basic iOS/Android app for on-the-go invoice viewing

QNE's strength is affordability. Pricing starts around MYR 29–49/month for single users with limited transactions. There's no setup fee and minimal onboarding friction, making it ideal for consultants, freelancers, and small retail operations just starting to formalize accounts.

SQL Accounting: The Established Contender

SQL Accounting is the veteran solution in Malaysia's SME accounting space. It's cloud-native, feature-rich, and deeply integrated with Malaysian tax and regulatory frameworks.

Core Strengths

  • Full-featured accounting: General ledger, accounts payable/receivable, stock management (inventory module)
  • GST compliance: Built-in GST calculation, real-time GST tracking, and SST (Service and Sales Tax) support for non-standard-rated items
  • Bank feeds: Direct integration with Maybank, CIMB, Hong Leong, and public Bank online banking; auto-reconciliation saves hours monthly
  • Multi-currency: Native SGD, USD, and MYR handling—valuable if you trade across ASEAN
  • Statutory reporting: Auto-generated Financial Reporting Template (FRT) for SSM filing; IRB-ready tax reports
  • User roles & permissions: Accountant-level controls for teams; audit trail logging
  • Mobile experience: Robust app with real-time dashboard and approval workflows

SQL's pricing is higher—typically MYR 99–299/month depending on modules and user count—but the feature density and local compliance depth justify it for businesses processing 50+ transactions monthly or managing payroll.

Pros and Cons at a Glance

QNE Strengths

  • Lowest cost entry point for solo operators
  • Zero learning curve—intuitive UI
  • No contracts; cancel anytime
  • Fast data import from Excel
  • Clean, minimal dashboard

QNE Limitations

  • No bank automation or feed
  • Manual GST reconciliation required
  • No payroll or inventory
  • Limited reporting depth
  • Weak multi-user support

SQL Accounting Strengths

  • Comprehensive feature set
  • Built for Malaysian tax & compliance
  • Bank feed saves reconciliation time
  • Scalable for growing teams
  • Premium support included

SQL Accounting Limitations

  • Higher monthly cost (3–5x QNE)
  • Steeper onboarding curve
  • Complex for micro-businesses
  • Requires stronger internet for stability
  • Mobile app still secondary to desktop

Which Should You Choose?

Pick QNE if:

  • You're a sole proprietor or partnership with under 20 monthly transactions
  • Budget is your primary constraint (under MYR 100/month)
  • You don't need payroll, inventory, or multi-user collaboration
  • Tax filing is handled by an external accountant
  • You're still learning accounting fundamentals

Pick SQL Accounting if:

  • You operate a registered company or partnership with growing transaction volume
  • You need real-time bank reconciliation to save time
  • You manage employees (need EPF/SOCSO payroll integration)
  • You track inventory or operate e-commerce
  • You require SSM FRT generation and IRB-compliant reporting
