Best Shopify Alternatives for Singapore SMEs in 2026

Quick verdict

4.7/5

While Shopify remains a strong platform, 2026 has brought competitive alternatives with better local support, lower fees, and native SGD/MYR pricing. WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and Xano lead the pack for Singapore retailers, offering superior payment gateway integration with DBS, OCBC, and UOB.

Why Singapore SMEs Are Looking Beyond Shopify

Shopify is reliable, but it's not always the best fit for Singapore businesses. Starting at SGD 46/month (basic plan in 2026), Shopify charges 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction for card payments, plus 3% for international transfers. For GST-registered businesses, these fees add up quickly—especially when competing with local platforms that offer native SGD pricing and direct integration with Singapore's banking ecosystem.

The real issue? Shopify's one-size-fits-all approach doesn't flex for regional nuances. Small retailers managing inventory across Singapore and Malaysia need platforms that understand cross-border tax, multi-currency checkouts, and local payment methods like PayLah!, Grabpay, and bank transfers. That's where alternatives shine.

Top Shopify Alternatives for 2026

1. WooCommerce (Best for WordPress Users)

WooCommerce remains the gold standard for Singapore SMEs running WordPress stores. Zero transaction fees, full control over your site, and seamless integration with local payment gateways like Instamojo and Xendit (which support DBS, OCBC, Maybank transfers).

Pros

  • No transaction fees—only hosting costs (SGD 8–60/month)
  • Complete customization and brand control
  • Works with 500+ payment plugins
  • Strong SEO foundations built in

Cons

  • Requires technical setup or developer hire
  • You manage security updates and backups
  • Slower initial launch vs. hosted platforms

2. BigCommerce (Best for Mid-Size Growth)

BigCommerce is gaining traction among Singapore retailers scaling beyond 5,000 SKUs. Pricing starts at SGD 70/month, with unlimited bandwidth and built-in multi-channel selling. Local payment support via Mollie and Adyen makes checkout frictionless for regional customers.

Pros

  • No transaction fees (Shopify charges 2.9%+)
  • Unlimited products and storage
  • Native multi-currency support (SGD, MYR, USD)
  • Strong B2B and wholesale tools

Cons

  • Higher monthly cost than Shopify basic
  • Steeper learning curve
  • App ecosystem smaller than Shopify's

3. Xano (Best for Custom, No-Code Solutions)

Xano is a no-code backend platform gaining adoption among Singapore fintech-adjacent retailers. Build custom checkout flows, loyalty programs, and subscription billing without coding—and pay only for what you use.

Pricing is transparent: from SGD 30/month (starter) to SGD 500/month (pro). For a lean operation, this is dramatically cheaper than Shopify Plus.

4. Wix (Best for Design-First Brands)

Wix eCommerce packages start at SGD 40/month and appeal to design-conscious brands. Built-in marketing automation, SMS, and email tools reduce the need for third-party software. GST compliance tools are available via integrations.

5. Square Online (Best for Omnichannel Retail)

Square Online integrates your online store with physical POS, inventory, and analytics. In Singapore, Square's payment processing is competitive, and you can add local payment methods via Adyen partners. Pricing starts at SGD 45/month.

Cost Analysis: Annual Spend for a SGD 100K Revenue Store

Assuming 2% average order value as platform fees:

  • Shopify Basic: SGD 552 (12 × 46) + SGD 2,900 (transaction fees) = SGD 3,452/year
  • WooCommerce + Xendit: SGD 360 (12 × 30) + SGD 1,200 (gateway fees ~1.2%) = SGD 1,560/year
  • BigCommerce Plus: SGD 1,200 (12 × 100) + SGD 900 (gateway fees) = SGD 2,100/year
  • Xano Pro: SGD 360 (12 × 30) + SGD 1,500 (custom development) = SGD 1,860/year

For a growing Singapore SME, switching to WooCommerce or BigCommerce can save 40–55% annually compared to Shopify—reinvesting that money into marketing or inventory.
