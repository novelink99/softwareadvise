Notion vs Confluence for Singapore SMEs: The Complete Comparison

Updated 2026-05-07 · 9 min read · Singapore

Our Verdict

Notion

For most Singapore SMEs on a budget, Notion edges ahead with superior flexibility, lower cost (SGD 10–20/month), and intuitive database features. Confluence remains better for teams deeply integrated into Jira or needing enterprise compliance, but Notion wins on value and ease of use.

Why This Comparison Matters for Singapore Teams

If you're running a startup or SME in Singapore, choosing the right workspace platform is critical. Whether you're documenting processes, managing projects, or building internal knowledge bases, Notion and Confluence are the two most popular options—but they serve different needs and budgets.

This guide compares both platforms specifically for Singapore-based teams, including pricing in SGD, integration capabilities, and real-world use cases that matter to Malaysian and Singaporean businesses.

Cost is often the deciding factor. Here's what you're actually paying:

Feature Comparison: What Each Platform Does Best

Notion: Flexibility and Simplicity

Notion is a blank canvas. You can build databases, wikis, project trackers, and content calendars all in one place. Its strength lies in:

  • Database architecture: Create relational databases with properties, filters, and rollups—ideal for building internal tools without code.
  • Customisation: Templates, synced blocks, and formulas let you design workflows exactly as you need them.
  • Multi-purpose: Use it for documentation, CRM, HR onboarding, knowledge bases, and more.
  • Learning curve: New users can be productive within days.

Confluence: Power and Enterprise Scale

Confluence is built for large teams and mature documentation practices. Its strengths include:

  • Jira integration: If your team uses Jira for development or project management, Confluence is a natural extension.
  • Version control: Advanced page history, branching, and review workflows suit documentation-heavy teams.
  • Permissions and governance: Space-level access controls, audit logs, and compliance features are built-in.
  • Structured content: Macros and templates enforce consistency across large teams.

Notion Pros

  • Much cheaper (50% less than Confluence)
  • Database flexibility is unmatched
  • Works offline; syncs when online
  • API is developer-friendly
  • Excellent for non-technical users
  • All-in-one tool reduces tool sprawl

Notion Cons

  • No Jira integration out of the box
  • Performance slows with very large workspaces (100+ pages)
  • Less granular permission controls
  • Limited collaboration features (no @mentions comments)
  • No native enterprise SSO (paid tier required)

Confluence Pros

  • Deep Jira integration for dev teams
  • Superior version control and audit trails
  • Advanced permissions and governance
  • Better for regulated industries (finance, healthcare)
  • Scalable for enterprise teams (100+ users)
  • Built-in SSO and security controls

Confluence Cons

  • Expensive for small teams (SGD 400+/month)
  • Steeper learning curve for non-technical staff
  • Requires Jira investment to justify cost
  • Limited database/CRM functionality
  • Less flexible for non-documentation use cases
  • Monthly minimum user commitments

Integration Capabilities

Both platforms integrate with popular tools, but in different ways:

Notion connects via Zapier, Make.com, and native integrations with Slack, Google Drive, and databases. You can embed external tools and create sophisticated automation workflows. For Singapore teams using cloud storage (Google Drive, OneDrive) or project tools (Monday.com, Asana), Notion plays well.

Confluence integrates deeply with the Atlassian ecosystem (Jira, Jira Service Management, Bitbucket). If your development team is already on Jira, Confluence becomes a powerful documentation hub. It also connects to Microsoft Teams, Slack, and enterprise tools.

Security and Compliance for Singapore Businesses

For Singapore SMEs handling customer data, compliance matters:

  • Notion: GDPR-compliant, encrypts data at rest and in transit. Good for general business documentation but lacks granular audit logs on free/team plans.
  • Confluence: SOC2, GDPR, and HIPAA certified. Offers detailed audit trails, IP allowlisting, and SAML SSO on enterprise plans—essential for regulated industries.

Both are suitable for Singapore business use, but if you handle sensitive data (financial records, customer PII), Confluence's compliance certifications provide stronger legal coverage.

Real-World Use Cases for Singapore SMEs

Choose Notion If You:

  • Run a startup with 5–15 team members and tight budgets
  • Need to build custom databases (CRM, inventory tracker, HR system)
  • Want one tool that replaces multiple subscriptions
  • Use asynchronous communication heavily (remote-first teams)
  • Are non-technical or have a tech-lite team

Choose Confluence If You:

  • Have a development team using Jira for project management
  • Need advanced version control and documentation governance
  • Operate in a regulated industry (finance, healthcare, legal)
  • Have 30+ team members and need granular access controls
  • Require enterprise features like SSO and audit compliance

Migration and Getting Started

Switching platforms mid-stream is disruptive. Notion exports cleanly to markdown and PDF, making migration straightforward.
