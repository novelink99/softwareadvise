Mailchimp vs Klaviyo Singapore: Which Email Marketing Platform Wins?

Updated 2026-04-26 · 9 min read · Singapore

Our Verdict

Klaviyo

For Singapore e-commerce and retail SMEs, Klaviyo edges ahead with superior automation, native SMS capabilities at local pricing, and deeper integrations with Shopify and Wix. Mailchimp remains ideal for budget-conscious startups and content creators who don't need advanced SMS marketing.

Mailchimp vs Klaviyo: Quick Overview

Choosing an email marketing platform is one of the most important decisions for Singapore SMEs. Both Mailchimp and Klaviyo dominate the market, but they serve different business needs and budgets.

Mailchimp is the household name—affordable, beginner-friendly, and available globally with SGD pricing options. Klaviyo is the growth-focused alternative, purpose-built for e-commerce and subscription businesses that need sophisticated SMS and push notification capabilities.

This guide compares both platforms across pricing, features, automation, integration capabilities, and support to help you decide which fits your Singapore business in 2026.

Pricing Comparison in SGD

Pricing is often the first filter for Singapore SMEs. Both platforms scale from free tiers to enterprise, but their structures differ significantly.

Feature Mailchimp Klaviyo
Free Plan Up to 500 contacts, unlimited emails Up to 250 contacts, 500 SMS/month
Growth Plan (10K contacts) ~SGD 40–60/month ~SGD 130–150/month
Pro Plan (50K contacts) ~SGD 120–180/month ~SGD 300–400/month (plus SMS costs)
SMS Pricing Additional paid add-on (~SGD 0.08–0.12 per SMS) Included, ~SGD 0.04–0.08 per SMS at scale
GST (9%) Applied on monthly plan Applied on monthly plan

Singapore SME Reality: If your company has fewer than 5,000 active contacts and minimal SMS needs, Mailchimp will cost significantly less. However, once you scale to 20,000+ contacts or need serious SMS marketing, Klaviyo's integrated SMS pricing becomes more economical than Mailchimp's add-ons.

Email Automation & Workflows

Email automation is where the platforms truly diverge. Mailchimp offers basic automation suitable for welcome series, abandoned cart, and birthday campaigns. Klaviyo is built for sophisticated, multi-channel workflows.

Mailchimp Automation

  • Simple automation workflows for common use cases
  • Basic conditional logic and segment triggers
  • Good for bloggers, newsletters, and service businesses
  • Limited personalization tokens and dynamic content

Klaviyo Automation

  • Advanced workflows with complex conditional branching
  • Native A/B testing and predictive send-time optimization
  • Deep behavioral triggers: cart abandonment, product browsing, order frequency
  • Integration of SMS and email in single workflow
  • Built-in analytics and revenue attribution

For a Singapore fashion e-commerce brand, Klaviyo enables you to send SMS reminders after a customer abandons a cart, then email them 3 hours later if they haven't completed checkout—all in one visual workflow. Mailchimp requires separate campaigns and manual integration.

Mailchimp Pros

  • Cheapest entry point for startups
  • Easy-to-learn interface
  • Excellent free plan with 500 contacts
  • Good email deliverability
  • Decent template library

Mailchimp Cons

  • Basic automation limits scaling
  • Expensive SMS add-on costs
  • Limited integration with Shopify (no native product sync)
  • Reporting lacks revenue attribution
  • Customer support primarily via chat/community

SMS & Multi-Channel Marketing

SMS has become critical for Singapore retail and F&B brands. Post-pandemic, SMS open rates average 98% compared to email's 25%, making it essential for time-sensitive promotions.

Mailchimp SMS: Available but treated as a separate add-on. You manage email and SMS independently, making campaign coordination awkward. SMS pricing adds up fast—at SGD 0.10 per message, a 10,000-contact SMS blast costs ~SGD 1,000 plus GST.

Klaviyo SMS: Integrated directly into workflows. Send email to 70% of your list, SMS to the other 30% who opened the email last time—all in one campaign logic. SMS costs are lower at scale, and local Singapore phone formatting is native.

For a Singapore F&B brand running a promotional campaign, Klaviyo's combined email + SMS approach is significantly more effective and easier to manage.

Integrations & E-Commerce Features

Most Singapore SMEs sell via Shopify, Wix, or their own WooCommerce sites. Integration depth matters enormously.

Wix Via Zapier only Native integration with SMS support
WooCommerce Basic sync via plugins Native with order and product data
Abandoned Cart Tracking Manual or via third-party app Native, with product recommendations
Post-Purchase Flows Limited Advanced (reviews, upsells, loyalty)

Customer Support & Learning Resources

Support quality varies. Mailchimp relies on community forums, knowledge base, and live chat (inconsistent response times). Klaviyo offers email support for all plans, dedicated account support on higher tiers, and a strong community academy.

For Singapore SMEs without dedicated marketing staff, Klaviyo's learning resources and email support are more valuable—you're not waiting for a chat agent; you get a response within 24 hours.

Who Should Choose Each Platform?

Choose Mailchimp if:

  • You're a startup with a budget under SGD 100/month
  • You run a content-heavy blog or newsletter business
  • You don't need SMS marketing right now
  • You're testing email marketing before scaling investment
