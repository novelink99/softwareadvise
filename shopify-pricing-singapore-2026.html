What's Included in Each Plan

The Starter plan (new in 2024, expanded in 2025–26) is ideal for first-time sellers testing the platform. You get a single product catalog, basic customer accounts, and the Shopify Point of Sale app—useful if you operate a physical retail space alongside online sales.

The Basic plan targets growing retailers with up to 5,000 monthly visits. You unlock staff accounts (up to 2), custom report builder, and standard email marketing integrations. For Singapore retailers, the Standard plan (SGD 155/month with GST) is the sweet spot—it adds automated discount codes, advanced analytics, and integration with major logistics providers like Singpost and Ninja Van.

Premium and above are reserved for operations doing over SGD 50,000/month in gross merchandise value. These unlock custom app development APIs, dedicated support, and advanced fraud analysis.

Transaction Fees & Payment Processing

This is where costs compound. Shopify doesn't force you to use Shopify Payments (their in-house processor), but most Singapore sellers do because third-party gateways add 2.2% transaction fees on top.

With Shopify Payments using SGD processing via DBS or UOB bank accounts, you pay:

  • Basic plan: 2.9% + SGD 0.30 per online transaction
  • Standard plan: 2.7% + SGD 0.30
  • Premium plan: 2.4% + SGD 0.30

For a SGD 100 sale on Basic, you lose SGD 3.20 (2.9% + 0.30) in fees before GST, leaving SGD 96.80. If you use a third-party gateway like 2Checkout or Stripe, add another 2–3% to that math.

GST & Tax Compliance for Singapore Sellers

As of 2020, Singapore's Goods & Services Tax (GST) remains at 9%. Shopify 2026 automatically calculates and displays GST at checkout if you register your store with a Singapore business address. For cross-border sales to Malaysia (6% SST), Thailand (7% VAT), or Indonesia (11% PPN), you'll need to configure Shopify Tax settings manually or use a third-party tax app like TaxJar (SGD 29+/month).

If your annual turnover exceeds SGD 1 million, ACRA requires GST registration—Shopify's built-in reports support this, but you'll likely need an accountant to file quarterly reconciliations. Most Singapore SMEs use Xero for this integration, which costs SGD 30–60/month additionally.

App Ecosystem & Hidden Costs

The Shopify App Store lists 8,000+ apps. Popular ones for Singapore sellers include:

  • Oberlo (dropshipping): Free–SGD 99/month
  • Printful (print-on-demand): Commission-based, no setup fee
  • Tidio (live chat): SGD 10–50/month
  • Klaviyo (email marketing): SGD 30–300/month
  • Judge.me (reviews): Free–SGD 25/month

A realistic full-stack setup (Standard plan + payment processing + 3–4 essential apps + email marketing) costs SGD 200–350/month before inventory, shipping, or marketing spend. Budget accordingly.

Comparing Shopify to Alternatives in 2026

WooCommerce (self-hosted on AWS/DigitalOcean) remains cheaper upfront (SGD 20–80/month hosting), but requires technical knowledge for updates, security patches, and backups. Wix and Squarespace are easier but less flexible—and their transaction fees (3.6% on Wix) negate any pricing advantage.

For Southeast Asian merchants specifically, consider:

  • BigCommerce: Similar pricing to Shopify Standard, better for high-volume sellers (over SGD 100K/month)
  • Lazada/Shopee integrated stores: Zero platform fees but locked into their ecosystems and algorithm-driven discoverability
  • PrestaShop: Open-source, requires DevOps knowledge; popular in Malaysia but underutilized in Singapore
