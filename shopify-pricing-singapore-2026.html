Shopify Pricing Plans in Singapore 2026

Shopify's pricing structure in Singapore hasn't drastically changed since 2025, but 2026 brings enhanced local payment integrations and competitive rates. All plans are billed in SGD and include 9% GST on top of the listed amounts.

The four primary plans available to Singapore merchants are:

  • Basic: SGD 36/month + GST
  • Shopify: SGD 104/month + GST
  • Advanced: SGD 324/month + GST
  • Plus: SGD 1,090+/month + GST (enterprise-level)

When calculating total costs, remember to factor in the 9% GST. A Basic plan merchant will pay SGD 39.24/month, while a Shopify plan subscriber pays SGD 113.36/month. For most growing Southeast Asian SMEs, the Shopify or Advanced plans offer the best balance of features and cost.

Transaction Fees and Payment Processing

Beyond monthly subscriptions, Shopify charges transaction fees that significantly impact your total cost of ownership. In Singapore, payment processing costs vary depending on your payment provider:

  • Shopify Payments (recommended): 2.9% + 30 cents SGD per transaction
  • Third-party gateways (Stripe, PayPal, 2Checkout): 2.0% + 30 cents SGD per transaction on Basic and Shopify plans; 1.0% + 30 cents SGD on Advanced and Plus plans

For a SGD 100 sale using Shopify Payments on a Basic plan, you'll pay SGD 3.30 in transaction fees. This is competitive for the region, though growing sellers should explore third-party gateway integration to reduce per-transaction costs at the Advanced tier.

Pros and Cons for Singapore SMEs

Strengths

  • Strong local integration with DBS, OCBC, and UOB bank feeds
  • Native SGD currency and GST-ready tax reporting
  • Competitive transaction fees vs. regional alternatives
  • Unlimited product listings and bandwidth across all tiers
  • Built-in multichannel selling (Facebook, TikTok Shop)
  • 14-day free trial with no credit card required

Weaknesses

  • Transaction fees add up quickly for high-volume sellers
  • No free plan; even basic costs SGD 36/month
  • Advanced features require upgrade to higher-cost tiers
  • Limited offline POS features compared to Square or Toast
  • Theme customization often requires developer hire (costs extra)
  • App ecosystem can lead to hidden monthly costs

Hidden Costs to Consider

While Shopify's published pricing is transparent, several additional expenses typically emerge after launch:

App Subscriptions

Most growing stores install 8–15 apps (inventory sync, email marketing, product reviews, SEO, analytics). Average app cost: SGD 50–200/month depending on volume and feature tier.

Theme Customization and Development

Custom design or advanced features often require a Shopify Partner developer, costing SGD 3,000–15,000+ for initial setup and ongoing maintenance.

Professional Email Hosting

While Shopify includes basic email forwarding, professional email (e.g., Google Workspace) costs SGD 8–15/user/month.

Paid Marketing and Tools

Shopify's built-in email and SMS marketing tools are basic; many sellers subscribe to Klaviyo or Omnisend (SGD 50–200/month).

Is Shopify Worth It for Singapore Sellers?

For most Southeast Asian SMEs, Shopify's value proposition in 2026 remains strong, especially if you're selling both locally and regionally. The platform's ease of use, local payment integrations, and ecosystem support make it ideal for bootstrapped founders. However, you should carefully model your transaction fees before committing.

For a typical Singapore merchant with 50 orders/month at an average value of SGD 80:

  • Monthly subscription (Shopify plan): SGD 113.36
  • Transaction fees (50 × SGD 80 × 2.9% + SGD 0.30): ~SGD 116.50
  • Total monthly platform cost: ~SGD 230

This is competitive with alternatives like WooCommerce (which has lower base costs but higher technical overhead) or regional platforms like Grab Shop or Lazada Seller Central.
