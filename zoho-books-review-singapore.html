Zoho Books Review Singapore 2026

8.1/10

Zoho Books is a solid, affordable choice for Singapore SMEs seeking integrated accounting software with strong GST compliance, multi-currency support, and seamless invoicing. Best for growing businesses under 50 employees; lacks some enterprise features found in Xero.

What Is Zoho Books?

Zoho Books is a cloud-based accounting platform designed for small and medium-sized businesses across Southeast Asia. Developed by Zoho Corporation (which operates across India, Singapore, and the region), Books combines invoicing, expense tracking, project accounting, and financial reporting in one integrated interface. For Singapore businesses, it offers native GST compliance, local currency support (SGD, MYR, PHP, IDR), and bank feeds from major local institutions including DBS, OCBC, and UOB.

Unlike desktop-only solutions, Zoho Books runs entirely in your browser—perfect for remote teams and distributed operations. The platform integrates with over 400 third-party apps, from payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal) to CRM tools (Zoho CRM itself), making it extensible for businesses with specific workflows.

GST Compliance & Local Banking Integration

Zoho Books' strongest feature for Singapore is its built-in GST compliance module. The software automatically calculates GST on invoices and expense entries, maintains audit-ready GST records, and generates the required GST-03 return format for IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore) submissions. The system tracks input tax credits and output tax obligations, essential for GST-registered businesses.

Bank feeds are pre-configured for DBS, OCBC, and UOB accounts, allowing automatic transaction imports and reconciliation. This significantly reduces manual data entry and reconciliation time. Many Singapore accountants appreciate this native local support—something less obvious in global platforms that require additional setup.

Key Features Overview

Invoicing & Estimates

Zoho Books makes invoice creation quick, with customizable templates, recurring invoice scheduling, and multi-language support. You can accept online payments directly from invoices (via Stripe, PayPal, or local gateways), and automate reminders for overdue invoices. For service-based businesses, the ability to track time and convert timesheets to billable line items is a valuable workflow.

Expense Management

Receipt capture is handled either through the mobile app or email forwarding. Zoho's receipt OCR reads vendor, amount, and date from images, reducing manual entry. Expense approval workflows support multi-level hierarchies, critical for organizations with spending controls. Currency conversion for international expenses is automatic and uses live rates.

Project Accounting

On Professional and higher plans, Zoho Books includes project profitability tracking, task-level cost allocation, and time billing. This is valuable for agencies, consulting firms, and contract-based service providers in Singapore that need to measure margin per engagement.

Financial Reporting

Standard reports (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow, Trial Balance) are generated instantly. Custom report builders allow you to slice data by department, project, or customer. Reports export to PDF or CSV; no seamless scheduled email delivery of reports (a Xero strength).

For most Singapore SMEs, Zoho Books offers better value at the entry level. If you're a startup bootstrapping costs, the S$36/month tier is attractive. However, if your business requires payroll integration with CPF contributions or you value faster support, Xero's slightly higher cost might be justified. Zoho shines for product-based businesses and service firms with project tracking; Xero leads in ease-of-use for non-accountants.
