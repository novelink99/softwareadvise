Lazada vs Shopee Seller Fees Singapore: Full Cost Breakdown

Updated 2026-05-09 · 10 min read · Singapore

Our Verdict

Shopee

Shopee edges ahead for most Singapore sellers due to lower commission rates on high-volume categories and more transparent fee structures. However, Lazada remains competitive for larger sellers with promotional budgets. Calculate your actual category fees before committing.

Why Seller Fees Matter for Your Bottom Line

If you're running an online business in Singapore, marketplace fees directly cut into your profit margins. On a SGD 100 sale, the difference between 5% and 8% commission equals SGD 3 lost revenue—that's 3% of your sale price gone. When you're processing hundreds of orders monthly, these percentages compound fast.

Lazada and Shopee dominate Southeast Asia's ecommerce landscape, each claiming millions of monthly active buyers in Singapore. But their fee structures differ significantly. This guide breaks down every charge—commission, logistics, advertising, and penalties—so you can calculate which platform maximizes your take-home earnings.

Lazada Seller Fees Explained

Lazada's fee structure is categorized by product type. Commission rates range from 2% to 20% depending on your category, with most mainstream categories sitting between 5% and 12%.

Commission Rates by Category (Singapore)

  • Electronics & Appliances: 6–8%
  • Fashion & Shoes: 10–12%
  • Home & Living: 8–10%
  • Sports & Outdoors: 10–12%
  • Beauty & Personal Care: 8–10%
  • Books & Media: 5–7%
  • Automotive Parts: 10%

Beyond commission, Lazada charges for:

  • Transaction Fee: 2% on top of commission (charged on the sale price)
  • Logistics Handling Fee: Free for Lazada-fulfilled orders, but 2–3% for seller-arranged shipping
  • Payment Processing: 1.5% if using bank transfer; free with Lazada Wallet
  • Advertising Platform (LazadaAds): Optional, but recommended budgets start at SGD 50/month

Shopee Seller Fees Explained

Shopee's commission structure is similarly tiered but tends to be slightly lower than Lazada across most categories. Rates range from 2% to 15%, with most categories between 5% and 10%.

Commission Rates by Category (Singapore)

  • Electronics & Gadgets: 6%
  • Fashion & Apparel: 9%
  • Home & Garden: 8%
  • Sports & Outdoors: 10%
  • Beauty & Skincare: 7%
  • Books & Media: 5%
  • Automotive Parts: 8%

Additional Shopee charges include:

  • Transaction Fee: 2% on top of commission
  • Logistics Surcharge: Free for Shopee-fulfilled orders; 1–2% for self-managed shipping
  • Service Fee (Premium Seller): Optional SGD 45/month unlocks better visibility and reduced commission by 0.5%–1%
  • Shop Ads: Similar to Lazada, optional but budget 50–100 SGD monthly for visibility

Shopee Advantages

Shopee Drawbacks

  • Premium Seller fee (SGD 45) adds up yearly
  • Competitive saturation in popular categories

Hidden Fees You Need to Know About

Return and Refund Processing: Both platforms charge fees if refund disputes escalate. Lazada charges a dispute resolution fee of SGD 2–5 per case; Shopee reserves the right to charge a 1–2% penalty on contested refunds.
