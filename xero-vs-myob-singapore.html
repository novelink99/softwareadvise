Xero vs MYOB Singapore: Which Accounting Software Wins?

Updated 2026-04-17 · 6 min read · Singapore

Our Verdict

Xero

Xero edges out MYOB for Singapore SMEs seeking modern design, superior cloud integration, and hassle-free GST 9% handling. MYOB remains solid for desktop-first users and businesses with complex multi-currency needs, but Xero's local bank feeds (DBS, OCBC, UOB) and mobile app deliver better overall value at SGD 25–50/month.

Overview: What Sets These Platforms Apart

Singapore's small and medium-sized business landscape demands accounting software that's intuitive, compliant with GST regulations, and integrates seamlessly with local banking infrastructure. Both Xero and MYOB have established themselves in the region, but they cater to subtly different user profiles.

Xero is a cloud-native platform founded in New Zealand and now a global leader. MYOB, also Australian-owned, has deep roots in Southeast Asia and offers both cloud and desktop solutions. For Singapore-based firms, the choice between them hinges on whether you prioritize modern, collaborative workflows or standalone desktop control.

Pricing & Value in SGD

Pricing is often the first filter for SME decision-makers. Here's what you'll pay:

Plan Xero (SGD/month) MYOB (SGD/month)
Starter/Basic SGD 25–30 SGD 20–25
Standard/Plus SGD 50–60 SGD 45–55
Premium SGD 80–100+ SGD 80–120

Xero's pricing is straightforward with no hidden setup costs, making it predictable for budget planning. MYOB occasionally charges installation fees for desktop versions, though cloud plans are more transparent. For a growing startup with fluctuating invoice volume, Xero's tiered model offers better scaling flexibility.

GST 9% Compliance & Tax Reporting

This is non-negotiable for Singapore operations. Both platforms handle GST 9% reasonably well, but Xero's implementation feels more integrated into its core workflow.

Xero's GST approach: Automatically calculates GST on invoices and expenses, with built-in IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore) reporting templates. The system flags GST-liable and GST-exempt transactions, making quarterly submissions to IRAS simpler. You can file GST returns directly from Xero's dashboard via third-party integrations.

MYOB's GST approach: Also compliant, but requires more manual configuration upfront. Tax code setup is less intuitive, and generating IRAS-friendly reports requires additional steps or third-party plugins. Accountants familiar with MYOB find it workable; first-time users often struggle.

For compliance-conscious SME owners, Xero's plug-and-play GST handling saves hours during reporting season.

Local Bank Feeds & Reconciliation

One underrated advantage Xero holds is direct integration with Singapore's three major banks:

  • DBS: Real-time feed available
  • OCBC: Same-day reconciliation supported
  • UOB: Full transaction sync enabled

MYOB supports bank feeds, but the integration is less seamless and updates are often delayed by 24–48 hours. For businesses processing daily transactions, Xero's live feeds eliminate manual data entry and reconciliation errors.

User Experience & Interface

Xero Strengths

  • Cloud-first, modern dashboard
  • Mobile app is excellent (iOS & Android)
  • Intuitive invoice & expense creation
  • Collaboration tools built-in
  • Multi-user access seamless

Xero Weaknesses

  • Steeper learning curve for non-accountants
  • Advanced reporting requires Xero Plus tier
  • Limited offline functionality
  • API customization needs developer input

MYOB Strengths

  • Desktop version offers offline access
  • Familiar to longtime Southeast Asian users
  • Competitive payroll integration
  • Multi-entity reporting stronger
  • Accountant portal is mature

MYOB Weaknesses

  • Interface feels dated (especially desktop)
  • Mobile app lags behind Xero
  • Steeper onboarding for new users
  • Bank reconciliation can be clunky
  • Fewer third-party integrations

Integrations & Ecosystem

Xero's app marketplace is extensive, with over 1,000 integrations. For Singapore SMEs, standout connections include:

  • Shopify (for ecommerce businesses)
  • Grab, Lazada, Tokopedia APIs
  • HubSpot and Salesforce CRM
  • Stripe and PayPal processors
  • Expensify for automated expense tracking

MYOB has a smaller app ecosystem with roughly 400 integrations. It covers essentials well (payroll, CRM, payments) but lacks the depth for omnichannel retailers or SaaS businesses.

If your business relies on multiple software tools, Xero's integration flexibility provides real competitive advantage and reduces manual data entry across platforms.

Support & Community

Both platforms offer email and chat support, though response times vary. Xero has a larger global community, meaning faster Stack Overflow solutions and more third-party tutorials. MYOB's support is solid but concentrated in Australia and Singapore, so queries outside business hours can take longer to resolve.

For solo entrepreneurs or small teams, Xero's self-service knowledge base and YouTube channel are more comprehensive.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Xero if you: Want a modern, cloud-native platform; operate an ecommerce or multi-sales-channel business; prioritize mobile access and real-time bank feeds; plan to grow and need easy scalability; value intuitive GST compliance.

Choose MYOB if you: Prefer desktop-first workflows with offline access; have an existing accountant familiar with MYOB; require complex multi-entity consolidation; operate in Malaysia or Australia as well (MYR/AUD pricing available).

The Bottom Line

For the majority of Singapore SMEs launched after 2015, Xero represents the modern default. Its cloud architecture, mobile-first design, and local bank integration align with how contemporary businesses operate. MYOB remains a competent alternative, especially for established firms with legacy workflows, but it's increasingly playing catch-up on user experience and integration breadth.
