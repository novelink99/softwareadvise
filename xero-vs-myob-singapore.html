Xero vs MYOB Singapore: Which Accounting Software Wins?

Updated 2026-03-29 · 9 min read · Singapore

Our Verdict

Xero

For most Singapore SMEs, Xero edges ahead with superior cloud reliability, better local bank integrations (DBS, OCBC, UOB), cleaner mobile apps, and more affordable entry pricing. MYOB remains strong for firms needing desktop flexibility or managing payroll in tandem with accounting—but comes at higher cost for comparable features.

GST 9% Compliance & Local Tax Reporting

Singapore businesses must manage the Goods and Services Tax (GST) at 9%, a critical feature for any accounting software. Both Xero and MYOB handle this well, but with different strengths.

Xero's advantage: Automatic GST calculation, seamless invoice tagging, and built-in IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore) compatibility. The dashboard makes it easy to see GST payable vs. recoverable in real time. For businesses managing multiple tax codes or cross-border sales within the ASEAN region, Xero's flexibility is superior.

MYOB's approach: Also compliant with IRAS requirements and handles GST reporting correctly. However, the interface feels less intuitive for tax-time reconciliation. MYOB excels if you're combining payroll with accounting, since both modules integrate tightly.

Bank Feed Integration for Singapore

One of the biggest operational timesavers is automatic bank feeds. Both tools connect to Singapore's major banks—DBS, OCBC, and UOB—but the reliability differs.

  • Xero: Direct integrations with DBS, OCBC, and UOB are well-established and synced daily. Transactions appear in your feed within 24 hours of posting. This speeds up bank reconciliation dramatically and reduces manual data entry.
  • MYOB: Also supports DBS, OCBC, and UOB, though some users report occasional sync delays. The reconciliation workflow is functional but less polished than Xero's.

For businesses processing high transaction volumes—e-commerce, service providers with multiple invoices—Xero's bank feeds are more reliable and responsive.

Invoicing, Expense Tracking & Automation

Day-to-day invoice creation and expense management are core tasks. Here's how they compare:

Xero Strengths

  • Invoice templates are highly customizable
  • Recurring invoices with automated payment reminders
  • Expense receipt scanning via mobile app (Xero Expenses)
  • Multi-language invoice support (useful for ASEAN trade)
  • Cleaner UI and faster invoice processing

Xero Weaknesses

  • Mobile app can be slow on older phones
  • Advanced expense categorization requires add-ons
  • Inventory features are basic (better via Shopify integration)
  • No desktop offline mode

MYOB Strengths

  • Tighter payroll integration (MYOB Payroll)
  • Desktop and cloud versions available
  • Longer history in Singapore market (brand familiarity)
  • Good for businesses managing employee rostering

MYOB Weaknesses

  • Pricing higher across all tiers
  • User interface feels dated vs. Xero
  • Fewer integrations with third-party apps
  • Less responsive mobile experience
  • Bank feeds sometimes lag

Payroll & Employee Management

If your business has employees and needs integrated payroll, MYOB's coupling with MYOB Payroll is seamless. However, Xero has partnered with leading Singapore payroll providers (like Guidepoint and ADP), offering flexibility without forcing you into a bundled solution.

For Singapore employers: Both handle CPF contributions, income tax withholding, and other statutory deductions correctly. MYOB's payroll integration is tighter (one interface for everything), but you'll pay a premium. Xero gives you the option to use your preferred payroll provider, keeping costs modular.

Support & Learning Resources

Xero has invested heavily in Singapore-specific support. Live chat is available in Singapore business hours, and there's an active community forum. MYOB offers similar support but with fewer Singapore-localized resources.

For SME owners in the region, Xero's video tutorials and academy platform are excellent. MYOB's support is functional but less comprehensive.

Scalability & Growth

If your business grows from SGD 1 million to SGD 5 million in annual revenue, both platforms scale. However, Xero's architecture is more modular—add apps and integrations as you grow. MYOB requires moving to higher tiers or bolt-on modules.

For fast-growing startups and scale-ups in Singapore, Xero's flexibility is advantageous. You can start lean (SGD 15/month) and add advanced inventory, projects, or integrations without major migration costs.

Who Should Choose Xero?

  • Startups and micro-businesses wanting low entry costs
  • Firms with multi-currency transactions (USD, MYR, etc.)
  • Businesses prioritizing mobile-first accounting
  • Companies using modern SaaS tools (Shopify, Stripe, Slack)
  • Those seeking the fastest bank reconciliation

Who Should Choose MYOB?

  • Businesses with 5+ employees needing tight payroll integration
  • Companies preferring desktop software alongside cloud
  • Firms with long-standing MYOB investments in Singapore
