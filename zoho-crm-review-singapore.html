Zoho CRM Review for Singapore SMEs

Updated 2026-04-25 · 9 min read · Singapore

Our Verdict

4.2/5

Zoho CRM is a robust, affordable choice for Singapore and Southeast Asian SMEs seeking enterprise features without enterprise pricing. Strong automation, local payment support, and flexible customisation make it ideal for growing sales teams. Best for: mid-market firms, startups scaling rapidly, and companies requiring deep integration with other business tools.

What is Zoho CRM?

Zoho CRM is a cloud-based customer relationship management platform designed to help sales teams manage leads, prospects, and customer interactions. Part of the broader Zoho suite of business applications, it competes directly with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Pipedrive in the Asia-Pacific market.

For Singapore SMEs, Zoho CRM offers exceptional value. Unlike Salesforce's enterprise-focused approach, Zoho is built for mid-market and small business budgets. The platform is available in SGD pricing, supports GST 9% compliance, and integrates seamlessly with local payment gateways and banking infrastructure used by Singapore firms.

Pricing in Singapore

Zoho CRM pricing in Singapore is straightforward and competitive:

  • Free Plan: SGD 0 – Up to 3 users, basic features, 5GB storage
  • Standard: SGD 44/user/month (billed annually) – Basic automation, email integration, 10GB storage per user
  • Professional: SGD 88/user/month – Advanced workflows, AI-powered insights, custom modules, 20GB storage
  • Enterprise: SGD 176+/user/month – Dedicated support, custom integrations, advanced security, unlimited storage

When accounting for GST 9%, a 5-person team on the Professional plan costs approximately SGD 4,752 annually. This represents strong value compared to HubSpot (starting at SGD 68/month) or Salesforce (minimum SGD 165/month for Sales Cloud).

Key Features for Singapore Businesses

Sales Pipeline Management

Zoho's visual sales pipeline gives you a clear view of deal stages. Users can drag-and-drop leads between pipeline stages, set deal forecasts, and track win/loss rates. For Singapore firms managing multiple customer segments—retail, B2B, corporate—this flexibility is essential. You can create multiple pipelines per product or market, a feature often locked behind higher tiers in competitors' platforms.

Workflow Automation

Automation is where Zoho shines. Create rules to auto-assign leads based on territory, automatically log emails to CRM records, send follow-up reminders, and trigger escalations. For growing teams where sales ops bandwidth is limited, this saves hours weekly. The Professional tier unlocks advanced workflows; the Free and Standard plans have limited automation rules.

AI-Powered Insights (Zia)

Zia is Zoho's AI assistant. It predicts deal closure probability, recommends next actions, identifies at-risk deals, and surfaces engagement patterns. For Singapore teams selling B2B SaaS or enterprise services, these predictive insights help prioritise high-value prospects and accelerate sales cycles.

Local Payment and Tax Integration

Unlike generic CRM platforms, Zoho handles Singapore-specific compliance. It supports SGD transactions, integrates with DBS, OCBC, and UOB via Xero or other accounting bridges, and generates GST-compliant invoices. If you're invoicing customers through Zoho, tax tracking is built-in.

Mobile CRM

Zoho's iOS and Android apps let your sales team update deals, log calls, and send proposals on the go. Critical for field sales teams across Singapore and Malaysia managing distributed client bases.

Best For

Zoho CRM works best for Singapore and Southeast Asian SMEs that fit these profiles:

  • Scaling startups: Affordable, flexible pricing scales with your team
  • B2B service firms: Consultants, agencies, and professional services benefit from custom pipelines and proposal workflows
  • Multi-product companies: Build separate CRMs for different product lines or regions without paying per-instance
  • Zoho ecosystem users: If you use Zoho Books, Zoho Recruit, or Zoho Desk, CRM integration is seamless and cost-efficient
  • Teams needing automation: Sales ops professionals can build sophisticated workflows without developers

Implementation & Support

Zoho offers free onboarding for new accounts, with guided setup and pre-built templates for common use cases. Paid implementation support is available (typically SGD 5,000–15,000 for full migration and customisation) for larger deployments.

Support in Singapore is available via email, chat, and phone during business hours. Response times average 24 hours for non-critical issues. Premium enterprise support includes a dedicated account manager.
