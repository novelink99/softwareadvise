Shopify vs WooCommerce Singapore: Which Platform Wins?

Updated 2026-03-27 · 9 min read · Singapore

Our Verdict

Shopify

For Singapore SMEs seeking fast setup and reliable compliance with GST 9%, Shopify edges ahead with managed hosting, built-in payment gateways, and local Stripe integration. WooCommerce wins for cost-conscious businesses with strong technical support who want complete control over their store.

Overview: Two Different Philosophies

Choosing between Shopify and WooCommerce as a Singapore ecommerce platform isn't about picking the "better" option—it's about aligning with your business needs, budget, and technical comfort level.

Shopify is a fully managed SaaS platform. You pay a monthly subscription (starting SGD 89/month), Shopify handles hosting, security, and updates, and you focus on selling.

WooCommerce is a free WordPress plugin that requires your own hosting, domain, and technical maintenance. It's powerful but demands more from your team. Hosting costs typically start at SGD 10–40/month, but time investment is significant.

For Singapore businesses operating under GST 9% regulations, payment compliance with local banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB), and multi-currency support (SGD, MYR, THB), both platforms deliver—but via different routes.

GST 9% Compliance & Payment Integration

Singapore SMEs must comply with IRAS GST regulations. Both platforms handle this, but differently:

Shopify's GST Support

  • Built-in tax rate configuration for Singapore 9% GST
  • Automatic tax calculations at checkout
  • Native integration with Stripe (supports SGD), PayPal, and 2Checkout
  • Seamless local bank payment gateways: Wise, Adyen (supports DBS/UOB accounts)
  • Automated order reports exportable for IRAS filing

WooCommerce's GST Support

  • Tax rates configurable via WooCommerce Tax plugin (free)
  • Requires manual setup and periodic updates for rate changes
  • Stripe plugin available (free) with full SGD support
  • Local payment gateways require separate plugin installation
  • Manual export needed for IRAS reconciliation

For Singapore compliance, Shopify's automated approach reduces accounting friction. WooCommerce works fine but demands more spreadsheet work.

Shopify: Pros & Cons

✓ Strengths

  • Zero hosting/server management
  • 99.99% uptime SLA
  • PCI DSS Level 1 compliance (security certification)
  • Integrated inventory, shipping, and analytics
  • One-click app installation (2,000+ approved apps)
  • 24/7 multi-language support
  • Abandoned cart recovery built-in
  • Mobile-first templates (fast checkout)

✗ Limitations

  • Monthly subscription is ongoing cost
  • Limited backend customization without code
  • Transaction fees (2.9% + 30¢ for Shopify Payments)
  • App costs add up (average store uses 6–8 apps)
  • Domain transfer out can be complex
  • No direct database access
  • Premium themes cost more than WooCommerce

WooCommerce: Pros & Cons

✓ Strengths

  • Zero platform fees (free plugin)
  • Complete control over data & database
  • Massive plugin ecosystem (70,000+ extensions)
  • Unlimited product listings
  • Full SEO customization
  • Scales infinitely with right hosting
  • No vendor lock-in risk
  • Lower transaction fees via Stripe Direct

✗ Limitations

  • Requires WordPress knowledge or developer
  • Hosting/domain costs (though lower)
  • Security updates responsibility on you
  • Slower out-of-box performance
  • No native customer support (forums only)
  • Plugin conflicts & compatibility issues common
  • Backup & disaster recovery self-managed
  • Steeper learning curve for non-technical founders
