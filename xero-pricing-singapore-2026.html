Xero 2026 Pricing Overview for Singapore

Xero's pricing structure in Singapore continues to evolve to serve the region's diverse business needs. As of 2026, Xero has maintained its four-tier subscription model, though base pricing and included features have been updated. All prices listed below are in SGD and exclude GST at 9%, which will be added at checkout for Singapore-registered businesses.

The main plans remain: Early, Growing, Established, and Premium. Unlike one-off software licences, Xero operates on a subscription model, meaning you pay monthly or annually (with a discount for annual commitments). This makes it predictable for cash flow planning and eliminates expensive upgrade cycles.

For Singapore SMEs handling regional business across Southeast Asia, Xero's multi-currency support and integration with local banks—DBS, OCBC, UOB—make it particularly valuable compared to purely local-only accounting tools.

What's Included in Each Tier

Early Plan (SGD $35/month)

The entry-level plan is designed for solopreneurs and freelancers. It covers unlimited invoices, expense tracking, and basic reporting. You get one user and access to mobile apps for on-the-go expense capture. Bank feeds for DBS, OCBC, and UOB are included, enabling automated bank reconciliation—a huge time saver for micro-businesses.

The Early plan does not include payroll features, making it unsuitable for businesses with employees. It also lacks project tracking and inventory management.

Growing Plan (SGD $82/month)

At this tier, you unlock two user accounts and can begin managing payroll for one employee, a critical feature for small team structures. Growing businesses gain access to bill management (useful for businesses with regular supplier payments) and more advanced invoice templates. CPF integration for Singapore payroll is available, streamlining employer contributions.

You're still limited to basic reports, but can generate profit & loss statements and balance sheets. Multi-currency transactions in SGD and MYR become easier to track without manual conversion.

Established Plan (SGD $165/month)

The mid-market tier expands user seats to five and employee payroll for unlimited staff. This plan is where most growing Singapore SMEs settle. You gain access to project tracking (essential if your business manages client work), inventory management (useful for retail or product-based businesses), and more detailed financial reporting.

Established also introduces workflow approvals—critical for governance—and integration with Shopify if you operate an e-commerce store. Fixed assets management helps businesses track capital purchases for depreciation calculations required by ACRA.

Premium Plan (SGD $249/month)

The top-tier plan includes everything in Established, plus five additional user seats (total ten), unlimited payroll, and advanced reporting APIs. Premium customers get priority support and can customize workflows heavily through the Xero API, making it suitable for highly specialized business operations.

This plan also includes advanced forecasting tools, useful for businesses needing cash flow projections for funding applications or board reporting.

Add-Ons & Optional Features

Beyond the core subscription, Xero offers paid add-ons that increase your total monthly cost:

  • Xero Practice Manager: For accounting firms managing multiple clients (not typical for individual SMEs)
  • Xero Projects: Enhanced project accounting and time tracking (SGD $30/month)
  • Xero Expenses: Streamlined expense management for teams (already included in Growing and above)
  • Additional User Seats: Beyond plan limits, typically SGD $20–40 per user/month depending on tier

For businesses in retail or F&B, consider whether integrating a POS system adds significant cost. Many third-party POS providers (like Toast or Square) connect to Xero but charge separately.

Real Cost Example for a Singapore SME

Let's say you run a growing marketing agency in Singapore with four staff members. You choose the Established plan at SGD 165/month. Here's your true annual cost:

  • Monthly subscription: SGD 165
  • GST (9%): SGD 14.85
  • Monthly total: SGD 179.85
  • Annual cost: SGD 2,158.20

If you add one extra user seat (SGD 30/month) and enable Xero Projects (SGD 30/month), your monthly bill rises to SGD 225 + SGD 20.25 GST = SGD 245.25, or roughly SGD 2,943/year.

This compares favourably to hiring a part-time bookkeeper (typically SGD 2,500–4,500/month) or using a local accountancy firm for outsourced bookkeeping, though those options may provide more personalized advice.

Is Xero Worth It in 2026?

For Singapore SMEs, Xero remains the best-value cloud accounting option. The 2026 pricing refresh has kept pace with inflation while delivering new features. Local bank integrations eliminate hours of manual work weekly. CPF payroll automation directly benefits businesses with Singapore staff. And the multi-currency, multi-location capabilities make it superior to purely offline or localized tools if you're expanding regionally.
