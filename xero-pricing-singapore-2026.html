Xero Pricing in Singapore 2026

Xero's pricing structure has remained competitive in 2026, making it one of the most accessible cloud accounting solutions for Singapore-based SMEs. All prices are displayed in SGD, with 9% GST added at checkout. The platform offers four main subscription tiers designed to scale with your business.

As of April 2026, Xero's entry-level plan starts at SGD 15 per month (before GST), making it the most affordable option for sole proprietors and micro-businesses just starting out. The plans scale up to accommodate mid-market enterprises with more complex invoicing, multi-currency, and project tracking needs.

What's Included in Each Plan

Early Plan (SGD 15/month)

The Early plan is ideal for freelancers, contractors, and sole proprietorships looking to formalize their finances. You get unlimited invoice creation, basic expense tracking, and the ability to connect up to five bank accounts. GST compliance is built in, so you can track and report GST liability accurately for ACRA requirements.

One notable limitation: the Early plan doesn't include project profitability tracking or time tracking features. If your business needs to allocate costs by project, you'll need to upgrade to Growing.

Growing Plan (SGD 45/month)

The Growing plan is where most Singapore SMEs land, especially those with 2-5 team members. It includes everything in Early, plus project tracking, time tracking (perfect for service-based businesses), bill payments, and purchase order generation. You can also invite up to five team members, each with role-based access control.

This plan is particularly popular with small accounting practices, digital agencies, and consulting firms managing multiple client projects simultaneously.

Established Plan (SGD 75/month)

The Established plan caters to growing mid-market businesses with 6-10 team members. Added features include advanced reporting, budgeting tools, and up to 20 team members. You gain access to custom fields, inventory tracking, and advanced approval workflows—essential if you manage stock or run a retail operation.

Premium Plan (Contact Sales)

Xero's Premium tier is designed for complex enterprises with substantial transaction volumes, multiple entities, or advanced integration needs. Pricing is custom-quoted based on your requirements. You'll likely work directly with Xero's enterprise team to discuss API access, custom development, and dedicated support options.

Annual vs. Monthly Billing in Singapore

Choosing annual billing can deliver meaningful savings. If you opt for a yearly subscription, Xero typically offers a 10-20% discount across all plans. For the Growing plan, that means paying SGD 450/year (or SGD 37.50/month equivalent) instead of SGD 540 on monthly billing—a saving of SGD 90 plus you save on GST calculations.

For cash flow purposes, many Singapore SMEs find monthly billing more flexible, especially when scaling. You can upgrade or downgrade at month's end without penalty.

Hidden Costs & Add-ons

Xero's published pricing covers core accounting functionality. However, there are optional add-ons and integration costs to consider:

  • Payroll: SGD 5-8/employee/month (essential if you have staff; handles CPF contributions, tax withholding)
  • Xero Practice Manager: Free for accountants managing multiple clients
  • Zapier/third-party integrations: May incur monthly fees depending on automation complexity
  • Advanced support: Usually included; Premium plans may offer priority support

The payroll module is strongly recommended for businesses with employees, as it ensures compliance with MOM (Ministry of Manpower) requirements and automates CPF calculations—a critical compliance step in Singapore.

Xero vs. Competitors in Singapore 2026

How does Xero stack up against other cloud accounting tools available to Singapore SMEs? Xero's main competitors include Wave (free tier available), QuickBooks Online, and local alternatives like Zoho Books.

Wave offers a free accounting plan but lacks project tracking and payroll integration—making it only viable for very small operations. QuickBooks Online pricing is comparable to Xero's Growing plan but has fewer local Singapore integrations. Zoho Books is typically SGD 10-30/month cheaper but lacks the same level of local bank feed support.

Xero's edge lies in its native integration with DBS NetBank, OCBC Digital, and UOB, plus its deep GST and ACRA compliance features built for Singapore regulations.

Getting Started: 14-Day Free Trial

Xero offers a 14-day free trial with full access to all features, no credit card required. This is the perfect opportunity to test bank feeds with your DBS, OCBC, or UOB account and see if the interface suits your team's workflow. Most Singapore SMEs can assess Xero's fit for their business comfortably within this window.

Whether you're a freelancer starting out (Early, SGD 15/month) or a growing team needing advanced features (Established, SGD 75/month), Xero's transparent pricing, local compliance features, and seamless bank integration make it an excellent investment.
