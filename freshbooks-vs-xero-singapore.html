FreshBooks vs Xero Singapore: Which is Best for Your Business?

Updated 2026-03-31 · 9 min read · Singapore

Our Verdict

Xero

For Singapore SMEs, Xero edges ahead with superior GST compliance, local bank integrations (DBS, OCBC, UOB), and competitive pricing in SGD. FreshBooks excels for service-based businesses and freelancers who prioritize invoicing and time tracking over deep accounting features.

Overview: FreshBooks vs Xero

Both FreshBooks and Xero are cloud-based accounting platforms trusted by thousands of businesses across Southeast Asia. However, they serve different business profiles. FreshBooks is optimized for invoicing, expense tracking, and client payments—ideal for consultants, agencies, and freelancers. Xero, meanwhile, is a comprehensive double-entry accounting system built for businesses that need full financial reporting, GST management, and multi-currency support.

In the Singapore context, where GST compliance at 9% is mandatory, and businesses often work across Malaysia (6% SST), Indonesia (10% PPN), and other ASEAN nations, this distinction matters significantly.

Pricing Comparison

FreshBooks operates on a tiered subscription model starting at around SGD 15–25/month (approximately USD 11–18), with plans scaling up based on invoice volume and features. The entry-level Early bird plan is suitable for new freelancers, while Plus and Premium tiers add staff roles, expense tracking, and advanced reporting.

Xero's pricing in Singapore begins at SGD 13/month for the Starter plan (up to 50 invoices/month), SGD 33/month for Standard (unlimited invoicing), and SGD 82/month for Premium (multi-entity reporting, fixed asset depreciation). For growing SMEs managing inventory, multiple tax jurisdictions, or staff costs, Xero's mid-tier offerings often represent better value.

GST & Tax Compliance in Singapore

Singapore's 9% GST and the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (ACRA) reporting requirements demand robust tax functionality. Xero has built-in GST tax codes that auto-populate based on transaction type—critical for businesses registering with IRAS (Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore). The software automatically calculates GST-inclusive/exclusive amounts and generates GST returns in the format IRAS requires.

FreshBooks handles GST adequately, but requires more manual configuration. Users must set up custom tax rates and ensure compliance manually, which increases the risk of errors during IRAS filings.

For businesses operating across Malaysia with the 6% Service and Sales Tax (SST) or other ASEAN jurisdictions, Xero's multi-jurisdiction tax template library is more comprehensive.

Bank Integration & Local Connectivity

One of Xero's strongest advantages in Singapore is native integration with major local banks: DBS, OCBC, and UOB. This means you can automatically import bank transactions, reconcile accounts in real-time, and reduce manual data entry by 80%. For a busy SME owner, this saves hours weekly.

FreshBooks integrates with international banks (Wise, Stripe, PayPal) and some regional players, but lacks direct feeds from Singapore's Big Three banks. You'll need to export CSV files manually or use third-party reconciliation tools like Zapier, adding friction to your workflow.

Invoicing, Expense Tracking & Payments

FreshBooks shines in this area. Its invoice templates are visually polished, customizable, and client-facing portals are intuitive. Time tracking is built-in, making it ideal for agencies billing hourly or in retainer models. Payment reminders, late fees, and recurring invoices are all seamless.

Xero's invoicing is functional but less visually refined. However, it includes expense categorization, receipt scanning (via the mobile app), and integration with receipt tools like Receipt Bank—useful for businesses managing multiple expense streams. Xero also connects to payment processors (Stripe, Square, GoCardless) for integrated payment links.

Integration Ecosystem

Both platforms connect to essential business tools. FreshBooks integrates with Zapier, Slack, Stripe, and PayPal, plus HR tools like Guidepoint. Xero's marketplace is larger, with connections to CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot), project management (Monday.com, Asana), e-commerce (Shopify, WooCommerce), and accounting add-ons (Hubdoc for receipt collection, Dext for expense scanning).

If you're running a Shopify store across Singapore and Malaysia, Xero's e-commerce connectors make inventory and multi-store reconciliation far simpler than FreshBooks.

Who Should Choose Each?

Choose FreshBooks if you: Are a freelancer, consultant, or agency billing clients hourly or on project basis. Need polished, branded invoices. Track time extensively. Don't require inventory or complex multi-entity accounting. Are based in a single market without GST complexity.

Choose Xero if you: Are a growing SME with inventory, multiple locations, or team members. Need strict GST and IRAS compliance. Bank with DBS, OCBC, or UOB (or another local bank). Operate across multiple ASEAN countries. Require financial reporting for lenders or investors. Process significant expense volume.
