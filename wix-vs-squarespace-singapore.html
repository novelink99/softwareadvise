Overview: Wix vs Squarespace for Singapore

Choosing a website builder in Singapore can be overwhelming. Two platforms dominate the conversation: Wix and Squarespace. Both are globally trusted, but which one truly serves Singapore SMEs best?

The answer depends on your budget, technical comfort, and business goals. Wix is more affordable and flexible. Squarespace prioritizes design and simplicity. In this guide, we'll break down both platforms to help you make an informed decision for your Singapore business.

Pricing: A Significant Advantage for Wix

When it comes to cost, Wix wins decisively for price-conscious Singapore businesses. Wix's entry plan starts at just SGD 7.50/month (during promotional periods, or SGD 10/month regularly), compared to Squarespace's minimum of SGD 14+/month when converted from USD pricing.

For a startup or freelancer operating on tight margins, this difference adds up. Over a year, you'd save SGD 84–132 with Wix—money that could go toward inventory, marketing, or staff. Both platforms include GST 9% (applicable to Singapore-based businesses), so factor that into your budget calculations.

Wix also offers transparent, tiered plans. Basic commerce starts at SGD 27/month, while Squarespace business plans hover around SGD 33+/month. Neither platform locks you into contracts, so you can upgrade or downgrade as your business scales.

Design & Templates: Squarespace's Edge

Squarespace is famous for one reason: design excellence. Every template is professionally crafted, responsive, and works beautifully on mobile. If you're launching a portfolio, creative agency, or boutique brand, Squarespace's aesthetics are unmatched.

Wix has caught up significantly. Its template library is vast and modern, with strong options for e-commerce, services, and blogs. However, Squarespace's designs feel more polished out-of-the-box. If design is your primary differentiator, Squarespace justifies its premium pricing.

That said, Wix's customization depth often exceeds Squarespace. You can inject custom code, add third-party widgets, and build more complex functionality without technical expertise. For Singapore SMEs that need to adapt quickly to market demands, Wix's flexibility wins.

Wix Strengths

  • Affordable entry pricing (SGD 7.50–27/month)
  • Extensive app marketplace (500+)
  • Strong e-commerce with payment gateways (Stripe, Adyen)
  • Better inventory management for retail
  • Local payment support (SGD, MYR)
  • Flexible customization via Velo (code)

Wix Weaknesses

  • Steeper learning curve for advanced features
  • Template switching can disturb layouts
  • Some apps have additional costs
  • Slower hosting vs competitors
  • Limited blogging tools vs dedicated platforms
  • Customer support (chat, email, no phone)

Squarespace Strengths

  • Outstanding template design library
  • Intuitive drag-and-drop editor
  • Excellent customer support (phone, chat, email)
  • Built-in analytics and SEO tools
  • Strong blogging platform integration
  • Professional email integration

Squarespace Weaknesses

  • Higher pricing (SGD 14–33+/month)
  • Limited third-party integrations
  • Weaker inventory features for retail
  • No local payment gateway options
  • Less flexible customization
  • Higher transaction fees on e-commerce

E-Commerce & Payment Gateways

Both platforms support online stores, but with different strengths. Wix integrates seamlessly with global payment providers like Stripe, PayPal, and Adyen—crucial for Singapore businesses. You can accept SGD, MYR, and international currencies. Inventory management is granular, with stock tracking and variants.

Squarespace also accepts payments but through fewer local gateways. Transaction fees are steeper (2.9% + SGD 0.30 vs Wix's 2.7% + SGD 0.30). For high-volume e-commerce stores, Wix's lower fees translate to meaningful savings. Squarespace is better suited for low-volume, high-margin boutique sellers.

If you need to integrate with accounting software like Xero (popular among Singapore SMEs for GST compliance), Wix's app marketplace offers robust plugins. Squarespace lacks comparable integration depth.

Performance & Hosting

Both platforms offer reliable hosting with global CDN coverage. Squarespace's servers are consistently fast, with minimal downtime. Wix's performance is solid but occasionally lags under heavy load—a concern for high-traffic campaigns.

For most Singapore SMEs running standard websites or small e-commerce stores, performance differences are negligible. Both load adequately on 4G connections common in the region. SSL certificates are free on both platforms, essential for SEO and customer trust.

SEO & Marketing Tools

Squarespace includes built-in SEO tools, auto-generated sitemaps, and structured data markup. Its blog platform supports clean URLs and category management. Wix has caught up with native SEO settings, but requires more manual configuration.

For Singapore businesses targeting local search (Google Maps, local keywords), both platforms allow schema markup for local business data. Neither is a replacement for dedicated SEO expertise, but Squarespace requires less hands-on configuration.
